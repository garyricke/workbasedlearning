# Field Experience — Strategic Foundation

## The Emotional Core

**Equity through access.**

The old system rewarded kids with connections—parents who owned businesses, family friends in desirable industries. If you didn't have those networks, you didn't get the experience. Mike returns to this point repeatedly: some kids were "lucky," others weren't.

Field Experience exists to change that equation. The school becomes the great equalizer—building the partnerships, providing the structure, creating pathways that don't depend on who your parents know.

Underneath the logistics and state guidelines, there's a deeper belief: **Every student deserves a real shot at discovering their future, regardless of their starting point.**

---

## The One Big Promise

**Know your path before you pay for it.**

The program promises students will leave high school with clarity—not just a diploma and a vague sense that "college is next." They'll have:

- Real workplace experience in a field they chose
- Professional connections they built themselves
- Proof of what they can do (not just grades)
- Certainty about whether this career is right for them

The promise isn't "we'll get you a job." It's bigger: **We'll help you figure out who you want to become—while there's still time to change your mind.**

Some students will discover they love engineering. Others will learn they don't want to be electricians after all. Both outcomes are wins. The alternative—spending years and tuition dollars before realizing you're on the wrong path—is the real failure this program prevents.

---

## Secondary Themes (for messaging hierarchy)

1. **Student ownership** — Kids apply, interview, and earn their placements. Just like the real world.
2. **Business value** — This isn't charity. Companies get motivated talent, fresh ideas, and a pipeline to future hires.
3. **Structured quality** — Unlike the loose old program, this has coaching, reflection, accountability, and a capstone.
4. **Safe exploration** — High school is the perfect time to try, fail, learn, and grow with support.

---

## Voice & Tone Direction

The transcript feels like a passionate educator explaining something he genuinely believes in. Not corporate. Not bureaucratic. Practical but hopeful.

**For students**: Speak to ambition and possibility. "This is your chance."
**For businesses**: Speak to mutual benefit and community investment. "Build your future workforce."
**For parents**: Speak to preparation and smart decisions. "They'll know before they go."

---

## The Line to Build Around

> "Get real experience. Find your direction. Start your career—before you leave high school."
